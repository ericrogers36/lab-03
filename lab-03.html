Exercise 3

Code

nobel_living <- nobel_alive %>%
  mutate(
    country_us = if_else(country == "USA", "USA", "Other")
  )

nobel_living_science <- nobel_living %>% 
  filter(
    category %in% c("Physics", "Medicine", "Chemistry", "Economics")
    )

ggplot(data = nobel_living_science) + 
  geom_bar(
    mapping = aes(y = country_us)) +
  facet_wrap(~ category) + 
  labs(x = "Number of living Nobel laureates",
       y = "Country",
       title = "Living Nobel laureates and the countries they were based in")

Narrative

The visualisation supports Buzzfeeds narrative that most living Nobel laureates were in the US when they won their prize, as the bar for the US is longer in every STEM category than the bar for laureates outside the US, suggesting many more of the laureates were based in the US. This disparity is greatest in Economics and smallest in Chemistry.
